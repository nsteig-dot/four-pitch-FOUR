# Global Blue Cross-Sell Opportunity Model

## Key Data Points (Primary Sources)
- Global Blue: ~80% global TFS market share, processing VAT refunds on ~$30B consumer spend
- 75,000 luxury goods merchants (TFS), take rate close to 3% on transaction value
- DCC business: ~€45M revenue, 96% gross margin — highly lucrative
- Global Blue total FY24/25 revenue: ~€506M, Sales in Store: €32.8-33.0B
- Revenue per merchant (TFS): ~€506M / 75,000 = ~€6,750/year (blended)
- Shift4 is modeling **$80M of revenue synergies** (confirmed at Susquehanna Conference Aug 2025):
  - Cross-selling Global Blue DCC into Shift4's US hotel/stadium merchant base
  - Converting a portion of Global Blue's retail customers onto Shift4 end-to-end processing
- Analyst (Bob Hammel) estimates: $0 in 2025, $25M in 2026, $65M in 2027, $80M by 2028

## The Cross-Sell Thesis
- 70,000 SMB luxury retail merchants globally (Lauber's stated cross-sell target)
- These merchants currently get TFS/DCC services from Global Blue but use OTHER payment processors
- The opportunity: bundle Shift4's full payment stack with Global Blue's TFS/DCC — one device, one provider
- Conversion uplifts revenue per merchant from ~$6,750 (TFS only) to ~$30,000+ (TFS + full payment processing)

## Revenue Per Converted Merchant (Annual)
- TFS commission: ~$6,750
- DCC spread: ~$2,500
- Payment processing (Shift4 end-to-end at 62bps on assumed $2M annual volume): ~$12,400
- Total per converted merchant: ~$21,650
- Incremental revenue vs. TFS-only: ~$14,900/merchant

## Key Risk: Enterprise vs. SMB Mix
- Per analyst research, ~80% of $500B cross-sell volume is with ENTERPRISE customers (harder to convert — Adyen already serves them)
- ~20% ($100B) with SMB — more likely to switch to Shift4's all-in-one terminal
- The $80M management model is likely conservative on the SMB cross-sell and optimistic on enterprise
- **Key test**: Watch H2 2026 merchant addition numbers — "several thousand/month" is the proof point
