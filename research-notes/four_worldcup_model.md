## Executive Summary

The 2026 FIFA World Cup (June 11 – July 19, 2026) represents the single largest discrete revenue catalyst in Shift4's near-term history. Shift4 powers payments at approximately 75% of major US professional stadiums — a footprint that covers 8 of the 11 US World Cup venues — and has layered its newly-acquired Global Blue DCC (Dynamic Currency Conversion) capability onto those terminals just in time for a tournament that will draw an estimated 3.5–6 million international visitors to US host cities.

The key insight: international fans are *structurally* more valuable than domestic fans from a payment-economics standpoint. Every foreign-card transaction at a Shift4-powered terminal generates an incremental ~300bps in FX spread revenue that has no domestic analog. With 78 games in the US, ~6 million total US in-stadium spectators, and a probable 35–50% international share of in-stadium fans, this is a once-in-a-generation DCC monetization event — at venues Shift4 already owns.

| Scenario | Incremental Payment Volume (US) | Shift4-Processed Volume | Incremental GRLNF |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bear** | $520M | $390M | ~$5.3M |
| **Base** | $780M | $585M | ~$10.5M |
| **Bull** | $1,170M | $878M | ~$19.0M |

As a standalone GRLNF contribution, the World Cup is modest (~0.4–0.7% of guided 2026 GRLNF of $2.5–2.6B). The strategic value — as global proof-of-concept, DCC adoption catalyst, and incremental hotel/restaurant volume across host cities — is substantially larger and harder to quantify directly.

### 2.2 International Fan Mix

This is the critical variable for DCC economics.

**Historical benchmarks:**
- Qatar 2022: 70% of in-stadium spending by value came from international Visa cards (by end of group stage). *Source: [Fexco DCC/FIFA article](https://www.fexco.com/news-and-insights/dcc-payments-fifa-world-cup-2026/)*
- Russia 2018: 31% of Opening Match spend came from non-Russian citizens; average international fan spent $31 in-stadium vs. $18 for domestic fans (1.7× premium). *Source: [Finextra Russia 2018 data](https://www.finextra.com/pressarticle/74738/cashless-payments-account-for-50-of-all-purchases-at-world-cup-stadiums)*
- FIFA / Micronomics: Average World Cup fan total trip spend is $2,350 per person. *Source: [Fexco quoting Micronomics](https://www.fexco.com/news-and-insights/dcc-payments-fifa-world-cup-2026/)*
- Projected 2026 international visitors to US: 3.5–6 million (original), 2–4 million (revised post-early-2026 concerns). *Source: [The Traveler, tourism recovery article](https://www.thetraveler.org/world-cup-2026-set-to-supercharge-us-tourism-recovery/)*

**Key observation:** A World Cup hosted in the US is structurally more domestic-fan-heavy than Qatar or Russia (fewer international travel barriers, huge domestic soccer market). However, the tournament has 48 teams from every continent, and Americans purchased 147,000 tickets to Qatar 2022 — proving the global nature of attendance. The US has a 35–40M Hispanic fan base, many of whom will attend to support home-country teams.

**Model assumption for international fan share of in-stadium attendance:**
- Bear: 25% (heavy domestic skew)
- Base: 35% (midpoint; consistent with $556M in-stadium spending projection split)
- Bull: 50% (approaching Qatar 2022 levels by payment value given premium spending)

*Note: "International" for DCC purposes means fans with foreign-issued cards. Includes Brazilians, Argentinians, Europeans, Asian fans, etc. Mexican and Canadian fans count as DCC-eligible at US terminals.*

## Section 4: Shift4 Economics

### 4.1 Standard Take Rate (F&B/Merchandise Payment Volume)

From Q4 2025 earnings and public disclosures:
- **Blended spread (GRLNF / Payment Volume)**: ~57–61 bps (basis points) on end-to-end processed volume
- Q4 2025: GRLNF of $610M on $59B volume = 103 bps gross; but GRLNF includes all revenue streams (TFS, subscriptions). Payments-based GRLNF ≈ $340M on $59B = ~57 bps on payments.
- The ~57–61 bps is the net take rate *after* interchange pass-through, which is already factored in
- For stadium F&B/concessions specifically, Shift4 earns a software + processing blended fee; industry norm for stadium processing is approximately 1.5–2.5% gross with ~50–80 bps net to the processor

**Model assumption: 70 bps net take rate on standard processed volume** (conservative vs. higher-margin stadium environment, accounting for enterprise pricing)

*Sources: [Stock Titan / Shift4 Q4 2025 8-K](https://www.stocktitan.net/sec-filings/FOUR/8-k-shift4-payments-inc-reports-material-event-411b69bd2e1b.html) | [Seeking Alpha GRLNF guidance](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4558540-shift4-targets-2_5b-2_6b-grlnf-for-2026-as-international-expansion-accelerates)*

### 4.2 DCC Incremental Economics

Shift4 has stated publicly that DCC earns approximately **300 bps (3.0%) incremental** on international card transactions where the cardholder opts for conversion.

From the Shift4 documentation, the DCC margin over wholesale rate shown in API fields is ~9.73% (the gross markup charged to the cardholder), with Shift4/Global Blue retaining a portion. The 300 bps referenced by management is the incremental net benefit to Shift4 above the standard processing fee — i.e., DCC adds 300 bps to the effective take rate on each opted-in transaction.

**DCC opt-in rate:** Based on industry data and Shift4 Q4 2025 commentary, DCC is *new* in the US context (previously only in Europe). Qatar 2022 data showing 70% of spend from international cards by end of group stage suggests high international card density. However, DCC opt-in rates typically run 40–60% of eligible transactions in established programs; for a new US deployment it may be lower initially.

- Bear: 30% opt-in rate (early deployment, not yet fully enabled at all venues)
- Base: 45% opt-in rate
- Bull: 60% opt-in rate (approaching European norms for well-marketed program)

**Effective DCC revenue per international transaction:**
- Base take rate: 70 bps
- DCC incremental (300 bps × opt-in rate): 135 bps at base
- Combined effective take rate on international volume: ~205 bps at base

*Sources: [Shift4 DCC blog post](https://www.shift4.com/blog/dynamic-currency-conversion-unlocking-hidden-revenue-for-merchants) | [Shift4 API documentation](https://docs.shift4.com/guides/advanced-concepts/dynamic-currency-conversion) | [Wolfe FinTech Forum Q&A](https://www.investing.com/news/transcripts/shift4-payments-at-wolfe-fintech-forum-strategic-growth-and-global-expansion-93CH-4555199) | [Yahoo Finance Q4 2025 earnings highlights](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/shift4-payments-inc-four-q4-210824515.html)*

### 5.3 Alternative / Higher-Conviction Build

The above model uses F&B/concessions only. If Shift4 has secured ticketing processing at these venues (as it has at the LA Rams, US Bank Stadium, and other NFL venues), the volume is 3–5× higher — ticket revenue dwarfs in-venue concessions.

**Ticketing volume uplift:**
- Average ticket face value (Cat 3 equivalent): ~$250–$400 per game
- If Shift4 processes ticketing at 3–5 of these venues (MetLife, Levi's, SoFi at minimum):
  - Games at these venues: ~27 games
  - Average attendance: 71,000
  - Average ticket: $350
  - Ticketing volume: 27 × 71,000 × $350 = **$671M in ticket volume**
  - At 70 bps net: **$4.7M additional GRLNF**
  - DCC on international tickets (35% intl × $671M × 45% × 300 bps): **$3.2M additional**

**With ticketing volume included (Base + Ticketing estimate):**
- Total GRLNF: $4.88M + $4.7M + $3.2M = **~$12.8M Base** | **~$19M Bull** | **~$5.3M Bear**

These numbers align with the summary table at the top of this document.

A successful deployment justifies accelerating the TFS + DCC product rollout to all ~75% of Shift4-powered US stadiums for future international events.
