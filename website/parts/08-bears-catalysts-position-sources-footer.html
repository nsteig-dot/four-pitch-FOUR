The Bear Case, Steelmanned

The entire bear thesis is one sentence: elevated leverage and an unproven acquisition. Both are temporary, measurable, and resolving on a public timeline. The more interesting question is what happens when they normalize.

COUNTER Acquisition was underwritten on international market entry and TFS leadership — not cross-sell. Global Blue contributes ~$200M+ EBITDA from TFS/DCC alone. FY26 guide embeds zero synergy — any progress is pure upside.
BEAR Stock has been “cheap” for two years — value trap

COUNTER The catalyst path is specific: May '26 (leverage peaked), Aug '26 (buyback complete + raise), Q4 '26 (sub-3x = institutional re-entry), Aug '27 (conv note repaid, shorts forced to cover).
BEAR Lauber is unproven as standalone CEO mid-integration

COUNTER Lauber has been President/CSO since 2018, running strategy through the entire hypergrowth phase. EBITDA margins held 49%+ through Global Blue integration. Isaacman bought $4M at the lows in March 2026.

True Downside Case

If EBITDA misses by 15% AND multiples compress to 5x simultaneously: EV = $5.1B, less net debt $4B = ~$14/share. This requires both operational failure AND multiple compression. Against a base case of $149–$284, the asymmetry is: −$28 downside vs. +$107–$242 upside.
