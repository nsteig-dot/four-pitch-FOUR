Why It's Cheap — The Market's Two-Line Bear Thesis

"3.7x leverage and a dilutive acquisition that hasn't proven its synergies."

That is the entire bear case. It is also the entire opportunity — because both concerns are temporary, measurable, and already resolving on a calendar that is public knowledge.

1
Elevated leverage (3.73x)

What the market sees: A company near its debt covenant cap (3.75x) that spent $1B buying back stock while simultaneously closing a $2.5B acquisition and making a TRA final payment. Peak leverage, minimum cash, maximum risk.

What is actually happening: Q1 2026 is deliberately the peak — three one-time cash drains hit simultaneously: Bambora ($84M), TRA final payment ($139M, gone forever), and $255M in buybacks. Management guided 3.73x explicitly to show they sized within the covenant. The leverage path from here is mechanically down every quarter for 8 straight quarters: 3.73x → 3.53x → 3.06x → 2.99x by Q4 2026 → 2.00x by Q4 2027.

The re-rating trigger: Sub-3x leverage = credit upgrade territory. Institutional mandates that currently can't hold FOUR due to leverage constraints become buyers. This is a scheduled event, not a hope.

2
Global Blue dilution concerns

What the market sees: A $2.5B acquisition that added leverage, brought a CFO departure, created messy GAAP comps, and has yet to demonstrate the cross-sell synergies management promised. Integration looks slow, the EBITDA contribution from Global Blue is dilutive to blended margins near-term, and the 70K SMB merchant cross-sell hasn't shown up in numbers yet.

The critical distinction the market is missing: The acquisition was underwritten on two things the market already concedes — (1) international market entry into 75 countries, and (2) becoming the world's largest tax-free shopping provider. Global Blue contributes ~$200M+ annual EBITDA on TFS and DCC alone, before any cross-sell. The cross-sell — 70K SMB luxury merchants, targeting "several thousand per month" by H2 2026 — is pure optionality. Management explicitly guides zero synergy in FY2026. Any progress is upside the market isn't pricing.

The re-rating trigger: H2 2026 merchant addition data. Lauber named this publicly. If several thousand merchants per month show up, the acquisition thesis is validated with hard data.

Why the Discount Is the Opportunity: The market is correct that both concerns are real. It is wrong about whether they are permanent. Elevated leverage during a peak integration period is not a structural flaw in a business with $500M of annual FCF and fixed-rate debt maturing well into the next decade. PitchBook data confirms the scale of the mispricing: FOUR's current market cap (~$3.1B) is approximately equal to its post-IPO valuation in June 2020 — when the company had $737M of revenue, minimal EBITDA, and operated in a handful of US verticals. Today it generates $4.2B of revenue, $970M of EBITDA, $500M of FCF, processes $209B of payment volume, and operates in 75 countries. The business grew 5× while the equity went nowhere.
