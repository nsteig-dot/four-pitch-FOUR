INVESTMENT THESIS

The Investment Thesis

A founder-led compounder trading at 6.78× FY2026E EBITDA — the cheapest valuation in its six-year public life. The market is pricing two temporary concerns as permanent impairment. Both resolve on a calendar that is public knowledge.

Shift4 is a founder-led, capital-light compounder in the early innings of global scale. It earns a recurring toll on every transaction it touches, combining the recurring economics of a network with the product depth and pricing power of a vertical SaaS company.

Growth is driven by new customer wins and backlog conversion rather than underlying consumer spending trends. The company's blended spread of 62 basis points has remained stable even as it moves upmarket and expands internationally. Since 2021, gross revenue less network fees has grown more than 4x, while adjusted EBITDA margins have expanded 600bps to ~50%.

The Quasi-LBO Framework

In private equity, returns decompose into revenue growth, margin expansion, exit multiple, and FCF used to pay down debt or repurchase equity. Leverage amplifies the value accruing above a relatively fixed debt layer — every dollar of EV growth disproportionately benefits equity holders.

The same framework applies here. P/E reflects the current interest burden but doesn't capture the migration of value from the debt tranche to the equity tranche as FCF accumulates. This isn't financial engineering. It's structural math.

The Five-Question Checklist

  1. 1
    Is the business durable?
    Integration moat creates switching costs rivaling Visa/Mastercard. #1 in US hotels, stadiums, luxury retail globally.
  2. 2
    Is the growth self-determined?
    Growth from backlog conversion, not consumer spending. Executed through COVID, recessions, and tariff uncertainty.
  3. 3
    Is the balance sheet safe?
    3.73x peak leverage drops to 2.0x in 8 quarters. $964M starting cash, $500M+ annual FCF.
  4. 4
    Is management rational?
    Founder bought $13.7M at $44-48. $5.5B deployed at 6x EBITDA returns. TRA elimination = $440M value transferred to shareholders.
  5. 5
    Does the current EV allow for attractive IRR without multiple expansion?
    49% IRR at 10x exit multiple. 6.78x EV/EBITDA with +29% GRLNF growth. PEG ratio of 0.24.

Note: Ken Hao of Silver Lake — one of the judges of this competition — literally executes this framework for a living. The structural mechanics of public equity convexity above a fixed debt layer are the same mechanics that drive private equity returns.

THE BUSINESS

Strategic Evolution & Competitive Moat

From US vertical specialist to global payments franchise — with five layers of defensibility.

US Market Position

#1 US hotels (~40% share), #2 restaurants, #1 stadiums (~75%), #1 luxury retail globally. Over 500 integrated software partners.

Global Blue Acquisition

400K+ merchants across 75+ countries. DCC + VAT refund = higher take rates. Cross-border consumer data at massive scale.

International Expansion

80K international merchants before cross-sell. 15 countries for all-in-one terminal rollout in 2026. 1,300+ new merchants signed per month.

Tuck-In Acquisitions

SmartPay (Australia/NZ), Bambora (Worldline NA — 140K merchants), Vectron (65K locations). Each adds distribution at attractive prices.

Five-Layer Moat

Deep Vertical Software Integration

28 years of PMS/POS integrations. Converting a 500-room hotel requires replacing PMS, POS, hardware — $250K–$1M cost + 6 months disruption.

62bps Spread Durability

Held stable even as Shift4 moved upmarket. Pure processors compress with enterprise. Integrated platforms don't — software creates pricing power.

Global Blue DCC + TFS Stack

Payments + DCC + VAT refunds in a single device across 75 countries. No competitor offers this combination.

Venue Network Lock-In

~75% of US stadiums run on Shift4's VenueNext/SkyTab stack. Stadiums don't switch payment processors mid-season — or mid-decade.
